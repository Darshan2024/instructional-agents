## Section 4: Understanding Software Engineering & SDLC

### Learning Objectives
- Describe key principles of software engineering, including core activities like requirements analysis, design, implementation, testing, and maintenance.
- Identify different SDLC models and explain their characteristics, advantages, and suitable use cases.

### Assessment Questions

**Question 1:** Which SDLC model emphasizes iterative development and frequent feedback?

  A) Waterfall
  B) Agile
  C) Spiral
  D) V-Model

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The Agile model promotes iterative cycles and continuous stakeholder feedback, enabling flexible adaptation to changing requirements.

**Question 2:** In the Waterfall SDLC model, how are the development phases typically structured?

  A) Iteratively with constant feedback
  B) In a sequential, linear order
  C) Cycles focusing on risk analysis
  D) Through rapid prototyping

**Correct Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Waterfall follows a strict sequential flow where each phase is completed before the next begins, with little room for iteration.

**Question 3:** What is a key advantage of the Spiral SDLC model?

  A) Strict documentation and fixed scope
  B) Easy implementation for small projects
  C) Effective risk mitigation through iterative cycles
  D) Simplified management without risk analysis

**Correct Answer:** C
**Explanation:** The Spiral model actively incorporates risk analysis in each cycle, making it suitable for complex projects with high uncertainty.

### Activities
- Compare the Waterfall and Agile SDLC models in a table, highlighting their differences in approach, advantages, and limitations.

### Discussion Questions
- Consider a project with rapidly changing requirements. Would the Waterfall or Agile model be more suitable? Why?
- How does choosing a specific SDLC model impact the development process and product quality?
