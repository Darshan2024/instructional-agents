### 3. Areas for Improvement

While the slides are excellent, there are a few opportunities for enhancement:

*   **Content Consistency:** The final Q&A slide introduces concepts like mutability of strings and the use of dictionaries, and the study advice slide mentions file I/O. However, these topics are not covered in the preceding review slides. This could create confusion about the scope of the midterm.
*   **Debugging Techniques:** The debugging section focuses on interpreting a traceback, which is essential. However, it could be strengthened by including proactive debugging strategies that students can use when they don't have a traceback, such as using `print()` statements to inspect variable states.
*   **Visual Engagement:** The content is heavily text- and code-based. While clear, some concepts (like list indexing or the function "machine" metaphor) could be made more memorable and intuitive with simple diagrams.
*   **Active Learning Prompts:** The presentation is structured primarily as a one-way delivery of information. Incorporating small, interactive questions throughout the review could increase student engagement and help them self-assess their understanding in real-time.
