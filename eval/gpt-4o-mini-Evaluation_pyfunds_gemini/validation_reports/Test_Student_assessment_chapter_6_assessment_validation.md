- **Variety in Assessment Formats:** While multiple-choice questions are informative, incorporating varied formats (like open-ended or scenario-based questions) could enhance engagement and assessment depth.

- **Visual Aids:** Consider adding diagrams or flowcharts, especially in sections discussing problem-solving processes and error types, as these can complement written instructions and further clarify complex ideas.

## Specific Recommendations
1. **Diversify Assessment Types:** Integrate various assessment formats, such as practical coding exercises, short essays, or collaborative group projects, to assess comprehension and application of material.
2. **Enhance Activity Instructions:** Provide clear, step-by-step instructions for each activity, outlining expected outcomes to guide students effectively through the tasks.
3. **Incorporate Visuals:** Use diagrams or flowcharts in sections discussing the debugging process or comparing error types to support different learning styles.
4. **Clarify Time Expectations:** Include suggested time commitments next to each section to help students manage their time more effectively.
5. **Facilitate Peer Review:** Encourage students to review each other's work on specific exercises, fostering collaboration and community within the learning space.
