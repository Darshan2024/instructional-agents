# Areas for Improvement
- **Question Clarity**: Some multiple-choice questions could be rephrased for clarity to avoid any potential confusion about correct answers.

- **Depth of Activities**: Certain activities could be more elaborated to provide clearer guidance on expected outcomes, especially for those that require descriptive written responses.
- **Assessment Validity and Reliability**: Ensure that questions do not inadvertently suggest misleading correct answers, such as the incorrect identification of testing types.

# Specific Recommendations
1. **Refine Question Wording**: Review all questions for ambiguous phrasing and refine them for clarity. For example, Question 2 in Section 1 about testing types could clarify what is meant by “mentioned in the slide”.
2. **Expand Explanations**: Increase the depth of feedback provided for the explanations, particularly for the questions with potential misconceptions; this would help deepen students' understanding of the subject matter.
3. **Enhance Activity Guidance**: Provide clearer guidelines for activities, such as sample structure or key points students should aim to cover, especially for written tasks.
4. **Peer Review Mechanism**: Introduce a peer review component for the activities to foster collaboration and provide additional feedback on students’ understanding.
5. **Evaluation Metrics**: Propose standardized metrics or rubrics for grading activities and discussions to ensure consistency in evaluation.
