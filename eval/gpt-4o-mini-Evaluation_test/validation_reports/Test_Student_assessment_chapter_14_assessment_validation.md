- **Engagement and Motivation:** The integration of activities and discussion questions promotes active participation and encourages students to reflect on their own experiences, making the learning process more engaging.

- **Diversifying Question Formats:** While multiple-choice questions are useful, incorporating varied formats such as short answers or peer assessments could enhance critical thinking and application skills even further.

- **Feedback Mechanisms:** While peer discussions are encouraged, including a structured peer review process could help students provide more constructive feedback to each other.

## Specific Recommendations
1. **Include Varied Assessment Formats:** Consider adding short answer questions or reflective prompts to encourage deeper thinking.
2. **Suggest Multimedia Use:** Offer practical tips or examples for incorporating visuals, videos, or interactive elements in presentations.
3. **Provide Additional Case Studies:** Add a section with examples of exemplary presentations to guide students in understanding the key elements that make a presentation effective.
4. **Establish a Peer Review Process:** Create a structured framework for peer evaluations that guide students on how to provide and receive feedback constructively.
