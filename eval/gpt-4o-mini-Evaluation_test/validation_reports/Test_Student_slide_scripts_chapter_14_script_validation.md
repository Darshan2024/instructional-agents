## 3. Areas for Improvement

- **Examples of Common Challenges**: Including brief anecdotes or personal accounts of how past students have overcome challenges in presentations could make the challenges more relatable and provide a motivational lift.

- **Visual Aids Section**: Add a focused subsection on the types of visuals recommended for specific types of presentations, along with examples (charts, images, videos) and how to use them effectively.
- **Summative Questions**: After each major section, consider adding a few reflective questions for the audience to prompt discussions or personal reflection on what they’ve just learned.
- **Feedback Mechanism**: Introduce a brief description of how students can seek constructive feedback from peers or mentors pre-presentation, and how to effectively integrate that feedback into their preparation.
